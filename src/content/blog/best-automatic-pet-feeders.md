## The Best Automatic Pet Feeders in 2025

Automatic feeders range from simple timed dispensers to smart devices that recognize individual pets. We tested five of the best to help you find the right one for your household. Here's what we found.

---

## 1. SureFeed Microchip Pet Feeder — Best for Multi-Pet Homes

**Price:** $169 | **Key Feature:** Opens only for the registered pet's microchip

The SureFeed doesn't schedule or portion food — it controls access. The sealed lid only opens when it reads your pet's implanted microchip. For multi-pet homes where one animal steals another's food or eats a prescription diet, nothing else comes close.

**Pros:** Foolproof access control, no subscription or wi-fi needed, 6-month battery life, sealed freshness
**Cons:** No automatic portioning or scheduling, small bowl capacity, doesn't track intake digitally

---

## 2. PetSafe Smart Feed 2.0 — Best for Scheduling Flexibility

**Price:** $189 | **Key Feature:** Up to 12 meals per day with precise portions

The PetSafe Smart Feed excels at customizable scheduling. Set up to 12 daily meals, each with exact portion sizes from 1/8 to 4 cups. The slow feed mode is great for fast eaters. It's the most flexible timed feeder we tested.

**Pros:** 12 meal slots, precise portioning, slow feed mode, 24-cup capacity, Alexa compatible
**Cons:** Dry food only, hopper lid doesn't lock, occasional wi-fi drops

---

## 3. PETLIBRO Granary Automatic Feeder — Best Budget Option

**Price:** $79 | **Key Feature:** Reliable basics at an accessible price

The PETLIBRO Granary does the fundamentals well without overcomplicating things. Set meals on a schedule, get low-food alerts, and feed remotely via the app. At $79, it's the easiest entry point into automated feeding.

**Pros:** Affordable, easy setup, reliable dispensing, decent 5L capacity, quiet motor
**Cons:** Less precise portions, basic app, no slow feed mode, dry food only

---

## 4. WOPET Automatic Feeder with Camera — Best Combo Device

**Price:** $109 | **Key Feature:** Camera + feeding in one device

The WOPET combines a timed feeder with a built-in camera and two-way audio. Watch your pet eat, talk to them during meals, and record clips — all from one device. Useful if you want monitoring without buying separate gadgets.

**Pros:** Camera + feeder combo, two-way audio, night vision, meal recording
**Cons:** Camera quality is mediocre (720p), smaller 4L hopper, app is clunky

---

## 5. Cat Mate C500 — Best No-Tech Reliability

**Price:** $45 | **Key Feature:** Simple timer, no wi-fi, never fails

For owners who just want food dispensed at set times without apps or connectivity, the Cat Mate C500 delivers. It's a five-meal timed feeder with ice packs to keep wet food fresh. No wi-fi means nothing to disconnect or update — it just works.

**Pros:** Works with wet and dry food, ice packs for freshness, dead simple, no wi-fi required
**Cons:** Only 5 meal slots, no remote access, manual programming, small portions

---

## Our Pick

**For most pet owners:** The PetSafe Smart Feed 2.0 offers the best combination of scheduling flexibility, portion accuracy, and smart home integration. If you have multiple pets on different diets, the SureFeed Microchip Feeder is the only real solution. Budget shoppers should look at the PETLIBRO Granary — it handles the basics without fuss.

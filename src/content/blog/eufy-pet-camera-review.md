## What Is the eufy Pet Camera D605?

The eufy Pet Camera D605 is a mid-range pet camera that combines 2K video, AI pet detection, and a built-in treat dispenser — all without requiring a monthly subscription. At $139, it sits between budget cameras like the Petcube Cam 2 and premium options like the Furbo 360. The standout selling point: every smart feature works out of the box with no ongoing fees.

## Design & Setup

The D605 has a vertical tower design with the camera up top and the treat hopper in the middle. It's functional but not subtle — think "mini appliance" rather than "sleek gadget." The white plastic housing doesn't blend into most decor easily.

Setup takes about 10 minutes through the eufy app. Connect to wi-fi, insert a microSD card for local storage, load treats into the hopper, and you're streaming. No account subscriptions or cloud plans to configure.

## Video Quality

At 2K resolution, the D605 produces noticeably sharper video than 1080p competitors. You can zoom in on your pet's face and still see clear detail. Night vision switches on automatically and performs well — you can clearly identify your pet's position and activity even in a dark room.

The fixed camera angle is the tradeoff. There's no pan, tilt, or rotation. The 160° wide-angle lens covers a good area, but if your pet moves to another part of the room, you can't follow them. Placement becomes critical — aim for a spot that overlooks your pet's favorite area.

## AI Pet Detection

The onboard AI distinguishes between your pet moving, a person walking by, and ambient motion (curtains, shadows). This means you get relevant notifications rather than constant false alerts. Detection is processed locally on the device — no footage leaves your home.

In our testing, pet detection was accurate about 90% of the time. The occasional miss usually happened when our cat moved very slowly or was partially hidden behind furniture.

## Treat Dispensing

The treat launcher works reliably with standard small treats (anything under 0.5 inches diameter). You trigger it from the app with a button press, and the treat pops out from the front of the device. Distance is adjustable — you can lob treats a few feet across the room.

The hopper holds about 30 small treats, which means refilling every few days if you dispense frequently. We'd prefer a larger capacity, but it's adequate for occasional use.

## Storage & Privacy

All video is stored locally on a microSD card (up to 128GB). No cloud upload, no subscription storage tier, no footage on anyone else's servers. For privacy-conscious pet owners, this is a major advantage. You can review clips directly in the app.

## The Verdict

The eufy D605 is the best value in pet cameras if you want treats and smart detection without a subscription. The 2K video is crisp, the AI detection works reliably, and you'll never pay a monthly fee. The fixed angle and small treat hopper are real limitations, but at $139 with zero ongoing costs, it's our top pick for budget-conscious pet parents who still want premium features.

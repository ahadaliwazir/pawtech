## Overview

The **Furbo 360** is the latest generation of the internet's favorite dog camera. It offers a 360-degree rotating view, 1080p video, two-way audio, bark alerts, and the signature treat-tossing feature — all controlled from your phone.

We used it for a full month while working away from home. Here's the breakdown.

## Key Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Camera | 1080p, 360° rotation, 4x zoom, night vision |
| Audio | Two-way with noise cancellation |
| Alerts | Barking, person detection, activity zones |
| Treat tossing | Flings treats up to 6 feet |
| Storage | Cloud (subscription) or local 24hr rolling |
| Smart Home | Alexa compatible |

## What We Loved

The **360° rotation** changes everything. Previous pet cameras gave you one fixed angle. The Furbo 360 tracks movement automatically — when our dog walked across the room, the camera followed. No more staring at an empty frame.

**Bark alerts with context** are smart. Instead of just "your dog barked," the app shows a 30-second clip of what happened before and after. Was it the mailman? A squirrel? You can see immediately.

**Treat tossing** is still magic. Our dog learned within 2 days that the Furbo clicking sound means a treat is coming. It's genuine interactive play when you're away. The tossing mechanism holds about 100 small treats.

**Video quality** is genuinely excellent. Sharp 1080p during the day, and the night vision is clear enough to see if your dog is actually on the couch (they are).

## What Could Be Better

The **subscription is pushy**. Without Furbo Dog Nanny ($6.99/month), you lose person alerts, activity zones, and cloud history. The camera works without it, but you'll see constant upsell prompts.

**Treat size is limited.** Only small, round treats work reliably. Anything irregularly shaped jams the mechanism. We had 2 jams in a month with appropriately-sized treats.

**It's tall.** At 8.8 inches, it's not subtle on a shelf. If aesthetics matter, factor that in.

## Who Should Buy This

- Dog owners who leave their pet home alone regularly
- Anyone dealing with separation anxiety (the two-way audio helps)
- People who want to monitor a dog walker or pet sitter
- Treat-motivated dogs (so, all dogs)

**Skip it if:** you have cats (the treat tosser is dog-sized), or you refuse to pay for cloud subscriptions.

## Verdict

The Furbo 360 is the best pet camera we've tested. Period. The rotating view, smart alerts, and treat tossing create a genuinely useful way to stay connected to your pet. The subscription is annoying but the free tier is still functional. At $199, it's not cheap — but nothing else combines this many features this well.

**Rating: 5/5** — The gold standard in pet cameras.

## The Best Pet Cameras in 2025

We've spent months testing pet cameras across every price range. Whether you want to toss treats from your phone, track your pet with AI, or just keep an eye on things for under $50, one of these five cameras will fit. Here's how they compare.

---

## 1. Furbo 360 — Best Overall

**Price:** $199 | **Key Feature:** 360° auto-tracking + treat tossing

The Furbo 360 remains the gold standard for pet cameras. Its rotating base follows your dog around the room, the treat launcher works reliably, and bark alerts notify you when something's up. Video quality is sharp at 1080p with solid night vision.

**Pros:** Auto-tracking, treat tossing, Alexa integration, excellent app
**Cons:** Expensive, cloud features need $7/mo subscription, treats can startle some dogs

---

## 2. eufy Pet Camera D605 — Best Value With Treats

**Price:** $139 | **Key Feature:** AI pet detection + treat dispenser (no subscription)

The eufy D605 hits a sweet spot: treat dispensing, AI-powered pet detection, and 2K video — all without a monthly subscription. Local storage via microSD means no cloud fees. The only tradeoff is a fixed camera angle (no pan/tilt).

**Pros:** No subscription needed, 2K video, treat dispenser, local storage
**Cons:** No rotating camera, treat capacity is small, fixed mounting angle

---

## 3. Petcube Cam 2 — Best Budget Option

**Price:** $49 | **Key Feature:** Full HD monitoring at the lowest price

If you just need to check in on your pet without spending much, the Petcube Cam 2 delivers. 1080p video, two-way audio, and basic motion alerts for $49. No treat tossing or auto-tracking, but it nails the fundamentals.

**Pros:** $49 price point, compact design, night vision, two-way audio
**Cons:** No treat dispenser, no pan/tilt, smart alerts need subscription

---

## 4. Blink Mini — Best Multi-Room Setup

**Price:** $35 | **Key Feature:** Ultra-affordable with Alexa ecosystem

Not a dedicated "pet camera," but the Blink Mini works perfectly for monitoring pets at just $35 per camera. Buy several to cover multiple rooms. Works seamlessly with Alexa and offers free cloud clips with a Blink Subscription plan.

**Pros:** $35 each, great for multi-camera setups, Alexa integration, compact
**Cons:** Not pet-specific (no bark alerts), no treat tossing, basic motion detection only

---

## 5. Wyze Cam Pan v3 — Best Pan & Tilt on a Budget

**Price:** $34 | **Key Feature:** 360° pan and tilt for under $40

The Wyze Cam Pan v3 offers motorized pan and tilt with motion tracking at an absurdly low price. It's not pet-specific, but the tracking functionality follows movement around a room effectively. Color night vision is a bonus.

**Pros:** 360° pan/tilt, motion tracking, color night vision, $34 price
**Cons:** Not designed for pets specifically, no treat function, Cam Plus subscription for AI alerts

---

## Our Pick

**For most pet owners:** The Furbo 360 justifies its premium price with auto-tracking and treat tossing — features that actually improve your pet's day, not just your monitoring. If budget is a concern, the eufy D605 gives you treats and AI detection without any subscription. And if you just need basic peace of mind, the $49 Petcube Cam 2 is hard to beat.

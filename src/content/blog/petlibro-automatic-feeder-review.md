## Overview

The **PETLIBRO Granary** is a Wi-Fi connected automatic feeder that holds 5L of dry food and dispenses up to 6 meals per day on a schedule you set from your phone.

At $69, it's one of the more affordable smart feeders. But does cheap mean unreliable? We tested it for 2 weeks with a very food-motivated tabby cat.

## Key Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Capacity | 5L (about 20 cups of kibble) |
| Meals per day | Up to 6 scheduled meals |
| Portion sizes | 1-50 portions per meal (adjustable) |
| Connectivity | 2.4GHz Wi-Fi, app control |
| Power | AC adapter + battery backup (3x D cells) |
| Food compatibility | Dry food only, 2-15mm kibble size |

## What We Loved

**Setup took 3 minutes.** Download app, connect to Wi-Fi, set schedule. No frustration, no manual-reading required.

The **portion accuracy** is impressive for the price. We measured 20 meals and portions were consistent within 1-2 kibbles each time.

**Battery backup** means your pet still eats if the power goes out. The feeder stores the schedule locally and runs on D batteries until power returns.

The **app is genuinely good**. Clean interface, reliable notifications when meals dispense, and easy schedule editing. Not what we expected at this price point.

## What Could Be Better

**No camera or mic.** You can't see or talk to your pet during meals. If that matters to you, look at the PETLIBRO Polar (same brand, higher price with camera).

**The hopper lid isn't airtight.** If you live in a humid climate, kibble near the top may go stale after a week. We'd recommend refilling every 4-5 days rather than filling to max.

**Cats can defeat it.** Our test cat figured out she could paw food out of the dispenser chute. Not a flood of food, but a kibble or two per attempt. The optional chute lock ($8 accessory) fixes this.

## Who Should Buy This

- Pet owners who travel for work 1-2 days at a time
- Anyone managing a pet's diet/weight
- Multi-pet households (buy one per pet)
- People who want smart features without spending $200

**Skip it if:** you want a camera built-in, or you feed wet food.

## Verdict

The PETLIBRO Granary punches well above its price. Reliable portions, great app, and battery backup make it a no-brainer for anyone who needs scheduled feeding. The lack of camera is the main trade-off, but at $69, we can't complain.

**Rating: 4/5** — Best value automatic feeder we've tested.
